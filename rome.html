Rome

The Eternal City

Quick Facts

Currency Euro (EUR)
Language Italian
Timezone CET (UTC+1)
Best Time to Visit April - June, September - October
Visa Schengen visa for non-EU visitors

Top Attractions

Colosseum

Rome's most iconic landmark, this ancient amphitheater once hosted gladiator battles and public spectacles. Book skip-the-line tickets in advance to avoid long queues. Consider a guided tour to learn about its fascinating history.

Vatican City & St. Peter's Basilica

Visit the world's smallest country and home to the Pope. Marvel at the Sistine Chapel's ceiling painted by Michelangelo, explore the Vatican Museums, and climb to the top of St. Peter's Basilica for stunning city views.

Trevi Fountain

Throw a coin into this magnificent Baroque fountain to ensure your return to Rome. Visit early morning or late evening to avoid the crowds. The fountain is beautifully lit at night, creating a magical atmosphere.

Pantheon

One of the best-preserved ancient Roman buildings, the Pantheon features a remarkable dome with an oculus opening. Free to enter, it's a masterpiece of Roman engineering and architecture. Visit during rain to see the dramatic light effects.

Roman Forum & Palatine Hill

Walk through the ruins of ancient Rome's political and social center. Combined ticket with the Colosseum. Allow 2-3 hours to explore the extensive ruins and imagine life in ancient Rome.

Spanish Steps & Piazza di Spagna

Climb the famous 135 steps leading to Trinità dei Monti church. The area is perfect for people-watching, shopping on Via dei Condotti, and enjoying gelato. Note: sitting on the steps is now prohibited.

Hidden Gems

  • Appian Way: Ancient Roman road perfect for cycling or walking
  • Trastevere: Charming neighborhood with narrow streets and authentic restaurants
  • Villa Borghese: Beautiful park with museums and gardens
  • Testaccio Market: Local food market offering authentic Roman cuisine

Getting to Rome

By Air

Rome has two main airports: Leonardo da Vinci-Fiumicino Airport (FCO) and Ciampino Airport (CIA). Fiumicino is the main international airport, while Ciampino serves mostly low-cost carriers.

From the Airport

  • Leonardo Express: Direct train from Fiumicino to Termini Station (32 minutes, €14)
  • Airport Shuttles: Shared shuttles to city center (€6-8)
  • Taxis: Fixed fare of €48 from Fiumicino to city center
  • Bus: Terravision and other companies offer budget options (€5-8)

Getting Around Rome

Rome is best explored on foot, but public transport is efficient and affordable:

Roma Pass

Consider purchasing a Roma Pass for free public transport and discounted museum entries. Available for 48 or 72 hours.

  • Metro: Three lines (A, B, C) connecting major attractions (€1.50 per ride)
  • Buses & Trams: Extensive network covering the entire city
  • Walking: Many attractions are within walking distance in the historic center
  • Taxis: Available but expensive; use official white taxis with meters
  • Bike/Scooter Sharing: Various apps available for short trips

Visa Requirements

Italy is part of the Schengen Area. Non-EU visitors typically need a Schengen visa, which allows travel within 26 European countries. US, Canadian, Australian, and many other passport holders can enter visa-free for up to 90 days.

Best Time to Visit

Weather Overview

Rome has a Mediterranean climate with hot summers and mild winters. The best times to visit are spring and autumn when the weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Seasonal Guide

April - June (Spring): Perfect weather (15-25°C), blooming flowers, ideal for sightseeing. Peak season begins in May. Book accommodations early.

July - August (Summer): Hot and crowded (25-35°C), many locals on vacation. Some restaurants close. High season with peak prices. Stay hydrated and visit attractions early morning.

September - October (Autumn): Pleasant temperatures (18-25°C), fewer crowds, harvest season. Excellent time to visit with good weather and moderate prices.

November - March (Winter): Cool and rainy (5-15°C), fewer tourists, lower prices. Some attractions have shorter hours. Christmas season (December) is magical but busy.

Peak vs Off-Peak Seasons

  • Peak Season: May - September (highest prices, largest crowds)
  • Shoulder Season: April, October (good weather, moderate prices)
  • Off-Peak: November - March (lower prices, fewer crowds, cooler weather)

Local Festivals & Events

  • Easter (March/April): Major religious celebrations, Pope's blessing
  • Rome's Birthday (April 21): City celebrations and events
  • Festa della Repubblica (June 2): National holiday with military parade
  • Christmas & New Year: Beautiful decorations, nativity scenes, special events

Budget Considerations

Summer months (June-August) see prices increase by 30-50% for accommodations. Book 2-3 months in advance for better rates. Winter offers the best deals, but some attractions may have limited hours. Shoulder seasons (spring/autumn) provide the best balance of weather, crowds, and prices.
