Madrid

The Heart of Spain

Quick Facts

Currency Euro (EUR)
Language Spanish
Timezone CET (UTC+1)
Best Time to Visit April - June, September - October
Visa Schengen visa for non-EU visitors

Top Attractions

Prado Museum

One of the world's greatest art museums, housing masterpieces by Goya, Velázquez, El Greco, and other Spanish masters. Allow at least 3-4 hours to explore. Free entry on certain evenings.

Royal Palace of Madrid

Spain's official royal residence, though the royal family doesn't live here. Explore opulent rooms, the armory, and beautiful gardens. Book tickets in advance to avoid queues.

Retiro Park

Madrid's beautiful central park, perfect for strolling, rowing on the lake, or relaxing. Don't miss the Crystal Palace and the Monument to Alfonso XII. Free to enter.

Plaza Mayor

The historic main square, surrounded by beautiful architecture. Great for people-watching, enjoying tapas at outdoor cafes, and experiencing the vibrant atmosphere. Visit during festivals for special events.

Reina Sofía Museum

Home to Picasso's famous "Guernica" and an impressive collection of modern and contemporary Spanish art. Combined with Prado and Thyssen-Bornemisza, they form Madrid's "Golden Triangle of Art."

Tapas Tour in La Latina

Experience Madrid's famous tapas culture in the La Latina neighborhood. Join locals for a tapeo (tapas crawl) from bar to bar, sampling delicious small plates and Spanish wines.

Hidden Gems

  • Mercado de San Miguel: Beautiful covered market with gourmet food stalls
  • Debod Temple: Ancient Egyptian temple relocated to Madrid, great sunset spot
  • Chueca Neighborhood: Trendy LGBTQ+ friendly area with great nightlife
  • Matadero Madrid: Former slaughterhouse turned contemporary arts center

Getting to Madrid

By Air

Madrid-Barajas Airport (MAD) is Spain's busiest airport and a major European hub. It has four terminals and excellent connections to the city center.

From the Airport

  • Metro Line 8: Direct connection to city center (45 minutes, €5-7)
  • Airport Express Bus: Direct service to Atocha and Plaza de Cibeles (€5)
  • Taxis: Fixed fare of €30 to city center (daytime) or €35 (night/holidays)
  • Private Transfer: Pre-booked cars available (€40-60)

Getting Around Madrid

Madrid has an excellent public transportation system:

Tourist Travel Pass

Consider purchasing a Madrid Tourist Travel Pass for unlimited travel on metro, buses, and trains. Available for 1, 2, 3, 5, or 7 days.

  • Metro: Extensive 12-line network covering the entire city (€1.50-2 per ride)
  • Buses: Comprehensive bus network, great for sightseeing
  • Walking: Many attractions are within walking distance in the historic center
  • Taxis: Readily available, use official white taxis with meters
  • Bike Sharing: BiciMAD public bike system available

Visa Requirements

Spain is part of the Schengen Area. Non-EU visitors typically need a Schengen visa, which allows travel within 26 European countries. US, Canadian, Australian, and many other passport holders can enter visa-free for up to 90 days.

Best Time to Visit

Weather Overview

Madrid has a continental Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and cool winters. Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant weather for sightseeing.

Seasonal Guide

April - June (Spring): Perfect weather (15-25°C), blooming parks, ideal for outdoor activities. Peak season begins in May. Book accommodations early.

July - August (Summer): Hot and dry (25-35°C), many locals escape to the coast. Some businesses close. High season with peak prices. Stay hydrated and visit museums during the day.

September - October (Autumn): Pleasant temperatures (18-25°C), fewer crowds, harvest season. Excellent time to visit with good weather and moderate prices.

November - March (Winter): Cool and sometimes rainy (5-15°C), fewer tourists, lower prices. Christmas season (December) is magical with lights and markets.

Peak vs Off-Peak Seasons

  • Peak Season: May - September (highest prices, largest crowds)
  • Shoulder Season: April, October (good weather, moderate prices)
  • Off-Peak: November - March (lower prices, fewer crowds, cooler weather)

Local Festivals & Events

  • San Isidro Festival (May 15): Madrid's patron saint celebration with concerts and events
  • Madrid Pride (June/July): One of Europe's largest LGBTQ+ celebrations
  • Veranos de la Villa (Summer): Open-air concerts and cultural events
  • Christmas Markets (December): Beautiful markets and festive atmosphere

Budget Considerations

Summer months (June-August) see prices increase by 30-50% for accommodations. Book 2-3 months in advance for better rates. Winter offers the best deals, but some attractions may have shorter hours. Shoulder seasons (spring/autumn) provide the best balance of weather, crowds, and prices.
