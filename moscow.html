Moscow

The Heart of Russia

Quick Facts

Currency Russian Ruble (RUB)
Language Russian
Timezone MSK (UTC+3)
Best Time to Visit May - September
Visa Tourist visa required for most visitors

Top Attractions

Red Square & Kremlin

The heart of Moscow and Russia's most famous square. Surrounded by iconic landmarks including St. Basil's Cathedral, the Kremlin walls, Lenin's Mausoleum, and GUM department store. The Kremlin itself houses cathedrals, museums, and the presidential residence.

St. Basil's Cathedral

Moscow's most recognizable landmark with its colorful onion domes. Built in the 16th century, this Orthodox cathedral is a masterpiece of Russian architecture. Visit the interior to see beautiful frescoes and iconography.

Moscow Metro

Often called "the people's palace," Moscow's metro stations are architectural masterpieces. Take a metro tour to see the most beautiful stations like Komsomolskaya, Mayakovskaya, and Novoslobodskaya, adorned with chandeliers, mosaics, and sculptures.

Bolshoi Theatre

One of the world's most famous opera and ballet houses. Even if you don't attend a performance, take a guided tour to see the opulent interiors. Book tickets well in advance for performances.

Tretyakov Gallery

Russia's premier art museum housing the world's best collection of Russian art, from medieval icons to 20th-century masterpieces. Allow at least 3-4 hours to explore the extensive collection.

Gorky Park

Moscow's most popular park, perfect for strolling, cycling, or relaxing. Features beautiful gardens, fountains, cafes, and various activities. In winter, part of the park becomes a large ice rink.

Hidden Gems

  • Izmailovo Market: Huge flea market perfect for souvenirs and antiques
  • Kolomenskoye Estate: Beautiful former royal estate with wooden architecture
  • VDNKh: Soviet-era exhibition center with impressive architecture
  • Arbat Street: Historic pedestrian street with shops, cafes, and street performers

Getting to Moscow

By Air

Moscow has three main airports: Sheremetyevo (SVO), Domodedovo (DME), and Vnukovo (VKO). Sheremetyevo is the largest and handles most international flights.

From the Airport

  • Aeroexpress Train: Fastest option (35-45 minutes to city center, RUB 500-1000)
  • Airport Buses: Direct service to metro stations (RUB 100-200)
  • Taxis: Official taxis available (RUB 1500-3000 to city center)
  • Ride-Sharing: Yandex.Taxi and Uber available (RUB 800-2000)

Getting Around Moscow

Moscow has an excellent public transportation system:

Troika Card

Get a Troika card for convenient travel on all public transport. It can be used on metro, buses, trams, and trolleybuses. Much cheaper than single tickets.

  • Metro: Extensive network with beautiful stations, very efficient (RUB 55 per ride)
  • Buses & Trams: Comprehensive network covering the entire city
  • Walking: Many attractions in the city center are walkable
  • Taxis: Use official taxis or ride-sharing apps (Yandex.Taxi, Uber)
  • River Boats: Scenic way to see the city from the Moskva River

Visa Requirements

Most visitors need a tourist visa to enter Russia. The process requires an invitation letter, completed application form, and passport photos. Apply well in advance (at least 1-2 months before travel). Some nationalities may be eligible for e-visas or visa-free travel for short stays - check current regulations.

Best Time to Visit

Weather Overview

Moscow has a continental climate with cold winters and warm summers. The best time to visit is late spring through early autumn when the weather is most pleasant.

Seasonal Guide

May - June (Late Spring/Early Summer): Pleasant weather (15-25째C), long daylight hours, perfect for sightseeing. White Nights in June with almost 24-hour daylight.

July - August (Summer): Warmest months (20-30째C), peak tourist season. Can be hot and humid. Many festivals and outdoor events. Book accommodations early.

September (Early Autumn): Mild temperatures (10-20째C), fewer crowds, beautiful fall colors. Excellent time to visit with good weather and moderate prices.

October - April (Late Autumn/Winter/Spring): Cold and snowy (winter: -10 to 0째C), fewer tourists, lower prices. Winter can be magical with snow-covered landmarks, but very cold. Some attractions may have shorter hours.

Peak vs Off-Peak Seasons

  • Peak Season: June - August (highest prices, largest crowds, warmest weather)
  • Shoulder Season: May, September (good weather, moderate prices)
  • Off-Peak: October - April (lower prices, fewer crowds, cold weather)

Local Festivals & Events

  • White Nights (June): Festival celebrating the longest days with cultural events
  • Victory Day (May 9): Major national holiday with parades and celebrations
  • Moscow International Film Festival (June): Major cinema event
  • New Year (December 31 - January 1): Massive celebrations, beautiful decorations

Budget Considerations

Summer months (June-August) see prices increase by 30-50% for accommodations. Book 2-3 months in advance for better rates. Winter offers the best deals, but be prepared for very cold weather. Shoulder seasons (May, September) provide the best balance of weather, crowds, and prices.
